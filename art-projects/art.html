My Art Journey

The 2000s

I don't have too many records of my art from this time period, but I did develop a passion for art early in life. Much of my art exists in long lost sketchbooks or between the margin lines of homework paper. Around this time, like many kids, I was enraptured with the characters and worlds within cartoons and video games, and I attempted to recapture their magic in my own amateur art. I even had a DeviantArt account where I'd post much of my art, but I sadly deleted the account and most of my early art is now lost media (a recurring theme with my art).

Early 2010s

My art begun to improve as I gained more interests in a diverse range of media. Beyond just cartoons and video games, I also began to read comics, both physical and digital. One prominent piece of media that exposed me to a wide range of artists and styles was Homestuck (please reserve judgement). It was one of my earliest introductions to the concept of fanart and fan work, which would have a profound influence on my art journey.

Late 2010s

This is where I delved deep into online fan communities, creating and sharing art for IPs that I was passionate about and gaining a small following, and where my art saw its biggest improvement. I was mostly sequestered to Tumblr, but I had the unfortunate habit of deleting my blogs once I gained too may followers. Some of my art is lost to time as I didn't think to archive some of it on a harddrive or on the cloud.

The 2020s

I unfortunately didn't have as much time during these past few years for art, but recently I've been rekindling my passion for it.
